THE CORVETTE C2

EXPLORE HISTORY

THE CORVETTE GOES SHARP AND STRONG

Sting Ray Precision. Mid-Century Masterpiece.

Produced from 1963 to 1967, the C2 Corvette, better known as the “Sting Ray”, was a dramatic evolution in both performance and design. It introduced independent rear suspension, quicker steering, and tighter handling, transforming the Corvette into a genuine sports car. Lightweight and agile, the C2 came alive on twisty roads and racetracks alike. Models like the 427-powered Sting Ray offered raw, visceral power, while the split-window coupe became a design icon. The C2 was bolder, faster, and sharper, it showed the world that Corvette was ready to compete on a global level.

A NEW ERA IN DESIGN

Short Run, Big Impact

The C2 introduced a look that was futuristic, aerodynamic, and completely original. The 1963 split-window coupe became an instant classic, combining artistry with speed. Hidden headlights, crisp lines, and a low-slung stance made it look fast standing still. Inside, the Sting Ray cockpit was refined, focused, and sportier than ever before. Every visual element was purposeful, balancing beauty with performance in a way no American car had done before.

RACE-READY

The Sting Ray that Turned Heads, and Corners.

With power outputs ranging from 250 to 435 horsepower, the C2 had muscle in every trim. The introduction of the Z06 package offered stiffer suspension, bigger brakes, and a larger fuel tank, targeting competitive drivers. Corvette’s racing efforts flourished during this time, building credibility in SCCA and endurance racing. Engineers focused on balance and braking, not just raw speed, which turned the Sting Ray into a real handler.
