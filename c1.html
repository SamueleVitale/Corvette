THE CORVETTE C1

EXPLORE HISTORY

THE FIRST STEP

The Beginning of an American Legend.

The C1 Corvette launched in 1953 as a stylish two-seater with jet-age curves and a bold vision. While its original inline-six engine and automatic transmission weren’t ground-breaking, the arrival of the small-block V8 in 1955 transformed it into a serious contender. Wrapped in a lightweight fiberglass body, an industry first, it offered a unique blend of innovation and elegance. By 1962, with fuel injection and up to 315 hp, the C1 evolved into a true sports car, establishing Corvette as Chevrolet’s performance flagship.

A STYLE ALL ITS OWN

Where Innovation met Imagination.

The Corvette C1 set a tone no other American car had before, graceful curves, wire wheels, two-tone paint, and wraparound windshields. Its design was unlike anything on the road, echoing post-war optimism and an adventurous spirit. The signature mesh grille and rounded rear fenders created a lasting silhouette that would inspire generations. It was instantly recognizable, unmistakably American, and would forever be an icon.

BUILT TO EVOLVE

Sytle Meets Speed

Though the earliest C1s struggled in sales, Chevrolet refined the platform rapidly. With each year came improvements, more power, tighter handling, and modernized styling. As performance grew, so did racing interest. By the end of its run in 1962, the Corvette had transitioned from a bold concept to a credible sports car. The C1 laid the foundation for what would become a decades-long legacy of power, innovation, and undeniable presence on the road.
