THE CORVETTE C3

EXPLORE HISTORY

THE CORVETTE THAT DEFINED AN ERA

Radical Curves. Unmistakable Presence.

The C3 brought show-stopping design to the Corvette nameplate, with dramatic fender flares, a long nose, and a tapered fastback rear. Inspired by the Mako Shark II concept, it was one of the most visually aggressive American cars ever made. Early C3s packed serious performance, with powerful small-block and big-block V8s—including the legendary 427. It was wild, loud, and built for straight-line speed. The C3 didn’t just turn heads, it rewrote what American muscle could look like and laid the groundwork for Corvette’s image for decades to come.

A SHAPE THAT STUCK

A Design Icon Born in the Era of Excess.

Few cars are as visually locked to a generation as the C3. Its sweeping curves and wide stance became a signature of 1970s car culture. The pop-up headlights, chrome bumpers, and removable T-top roof panels created a distinct silhouette that captured the imagination of enthusiasts around the world. Even as emissions standards and fuel regulations tightened, the C3 kept its muscular looks, reminding everyone that a Corvette was never meant to blend in, it was meant to stand out.

EVOLVING SPIRIT

Bold Design, Immense Power

The C3 lasted for 15 years, the longest run of any Corvette generation. During its time, it evolved from brutal muscle to refined cruiser. Powertrains were tamed over the years due to fuel crises and emissions laws, but the Corvette identity remained. Handling improved, interiors became more driver-friendly, and special editions like the L82 and Collector’s Edition added performance and prestige. It was a car that aged with its time, never losing the flash and freedom that defined it from the start.
